About me

Hello! My name is Matthew Shan and I am a Computer Science major at Grand Valley State University. An interesting fact about myself is that both my parents are Chinese, whereas I was born in Canada and grew up in West Michigan. Some of my interests include playing music (such as piano, guitar, and ukulele), playing strategy games, and of course, coding!

Why Computer Science? [Download PDF Version]

  Computer Science is a very versatile field and technology has become an integral part of our culture due to its wide range of uses. Personally, I view technology as a tool to improve the quality of people’s life. One example is the invention of the printing press. The printing press allowed books to be mass produced, while maintaining an affordable price. This invention allowed the common folks to read, which gave everyone a chance to educate themselves. Another relevant example is the self-driving or autonomous car. I recently read an article from The Washington Post that describes a blind man who was given the opportunity to test one of Google self-driving cars. Although the self-driving car is not yet ready for public use, it has demonstrated its future potentials. That being said, how could I personally use my degree in Computer Science for technological advancement? I want to use my future expertise to help others. Currently, I am considering three different potential career choices: Software Development, Cyber Security, or Teaching.

  Software Development is one of my top career choices that I am considering, because I find it very exciting. Today, everyone uses software in their daily routines, ranging from the alarm clocks, emails, GPS, the operating systems on smartphones, social media, and so on. All of those technologies are applied into our lives to satisfy our basic needs. Without the help of those technologies, it would be either difficult to achieve our daily tasks. Software even has the ability to enable us to perform what was previously seen as impossible. As a programmer, I find that it is a joy to create something for practical use, not only because it is satisfying, but also because somebody else could use it to make their own lives easier.

  Another career choice that I am considering is Cyber Security. This career is very important in the field of Computer Science. Everybody's sensitive personal data is stored in a database, ranging from banking information to social security numbers. It is important that a security expert does his best to insure that this information will not be compromised. Unfortunately, as technology advances, so will illegal methods to exploit it. Security experts must stay up-to-date with secure practices in order to prevent hackers from compromising users’ data. If I decide with this career choice, I would feel proud of knowing each step for the job to work towards the protection of millions.

  The final potential career choice in my mind is to teach. This will be an option that I could consider after working for a company for a decade or so. With this rich experience in a company, I would like to give it back to the community. I have always admired my instructors in different ways, especially those who teach Computer Science. While those instructors could have picked a different and perhaps more financially stable career, they have chosen to impart their knowledge and experience to others. This is something that I deeply respect, and I look up to the instructors as a role model of our society. If I decide to pursue a similar career, I hope that I could give back to the community for what those instructors have provided me along with some of my own expertise and experiences in the field.

 After I graduate from college, I plan to use my degree to itself fullest. Not only do I want to do something I really enjoy, but also I want to be productive in society and be helpful in terms of improving the quality of lives of others. As we see, the past and present have demonstrated how important technology has been in assisting our daily routines. By taking on the career of either Software Development, or Cyber Security, or Teaching, I believe that I can achieve my own goal of assisting others and that I can be a contributing member of our own society.

My Journey in Detail

Classes in current semester: Winter 2018

CIS 241: System level programming and Utilities

CIS 241 is one of the classes I can take after CIS 163. In this class, we learn the programming language C and use of the operating system Linux. I always enjoy learning a new language and new strategies used in programming. I have always wanted to familiarize myself with the Linux operating system. I have heard several good things about it and this class is the first time I have a chance to work with it.

COM 201: Speech

Speech is a bit different from my other classes; however, I recognize its importance and its values. For anything that human beings do together, it is vital that we communicate properly. In the field of Computer Science, communication is key, as most work is accomplished through teamwork. Even if a person is an excellent coder, yet, he lack communication skill, it would be difficult to successfully complete a project with others.

MTH 225: Discrete Structures 1

Discrete Structures 1 is a class centered on applying mathematical concepts to Computer Science. In this class, we have been given the opportunity of implementing mathematical functions in the Python programming language. After learning this language, I now have a bit of an appreciation for the language. I look forward to what the rest of the semester will offer.

HNR 278/279 History of Science 3 & 4

This is the second semester of my Honors Sequence. As mentioned below , I believe that it is important for me to understand the scientists in history in order to contribute to future science. In this class, topics that are covered range from the Renaissance era to the Romanticism era. While I enjoyed learning the thoughts of great thinkers such as Aristotle, Dante, and Aquinas during the previous semester, I look forward to learning more about how thinkers of the early modern era had shaped today’s way of thinking.

Classes in first semester: Fall 2017

CIS 163: Computer Science II.

CIS 163 was the first Computer Science class that I took at Grand Valley State University. In the High School, I had coding experience from AP Computer Science, Web and Game Development, and learning in my free time. While I have taken a few coding classes in the past, I have found myself in awe of the passion all my coding teachers have demonstrated. While I have noticed that every professor or teacher who taught me has a passion in their fields, I find that there is something special about Computer Science instructors. Those instructors had many different choices in terms of career paths, yet they have chosen the humble jobs as guides for the next generation of coders.

HNR 258/259: History of Science 1 & 2

History of Science was the Honors Sequence that I choose for this year. I took interest in the History of Science, because I wanted to learn about the thought processes of great thinkers before our time, to whom we are all in debt to. I believe that studying great scientists and philosophers can be quite beneficial in the advancement of technology and may serve as useful knowledge in my future career. This course covers a wide range from Ancient Greece to the Protestant Reformation. Even after one semester of this class, I find myself being able to appreciate several sides of an argument.

CHI 201: Intermediate Chinese 1

Chinese is a subject field that I am considering to take on as a minor. As a U.S. citizen of Chinese descent, I think it is important for me to better myself in both my parents’ culture and language. While I can speak functional Chinese, I would like to improve my reading/writing skills. After taking this class, my perspective on my ancestor’s culture has widened. I am definitely considering to study abroad at least one time during my college years, while one of the locations I am interested in is China. What touches my heart is the diligence and commitment that other Grand Valley students put into learning cultures and languages. It was amazing to see people of all backgrounds appreciating the other’s cultures.

Potential Classes for next semester: Fall 2018

CIS 263: Data Structures & Algorithms

From what I know of CIS 263, this class teaches new data structures and algorithms in C++. I have worked with C++ in my high school Web and Game Development course so I am quite excited to go in-depth with the language.

MTH 202: Calculus II

Mathematics is a subject that I have always enjoyed. Before college, mathematics was my favorite subject. By the time I got to high school, I experienced coding for the first time and realized that I could apply mathematical thinking to Computer Science. In this class, I look forward to learning more about integrals and their applications in the real world.

MTH 325: Discrete Structures II

nce again, I am excited to further carry on my skills in mathematics to the next course of Discrete Structures II. My hope is that I can use a language such as Python again to help me to learn more in this class.

Undecided Honors College/ General Education course

While currently I do not know which class I will take as a general education course, I still look forward to whatever class I may take. I strongly believe that it is important to learn a subject outside of my own field in order to widen my knowledge and perspective.
